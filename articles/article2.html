Top 10 Superfoods for a Stronger Immune System

The foods we eat are the primary source of the raw materials our immune cells need to defend our bodies. Incorporating superfoods—foods exceptionally high in nutrients—into our diet is a proactive way to maintain peak health.

Citrus fruits like oranges and grapefruits are legendary for their high Vitamin C content. Vitamin C is thought to increase the production of white blood cells, which are key to fighting off infections during the cold season.

Garlic is found in almost every cuisine in the world. It adds a little zing to food and it’s a must-have for your health. Early civilizations recognized its value in fighting infections, and research now points to its sulfur-containing compounds.

Ginger is another ingredient many turn to after getting sick. Ginger may help decrease inflammation, which can help reduce a sore throat and inflammatory illnesses. It may also help with nausea and chronic pain as well.

Spinach made our list not just because it’s rich in vitamin C — it’s also packed with numerous antioxidants and beta carotene, which may both increase the infection-fighting ability of our immune systems.

Yogurt can also be a great source of Vitamin D. Vitamin D helps regulate the immune system and is thought to boost our body’s natural defenses against diseases. Try to select plain yogurts rather than the kind that are pre-flavored and loaded with sugar.

Almonds, being a fat-soluble vitamin, requires the presence of fat to be absorbed properly. Nuts, such as almonds, are packed with the vitamin and also have healthy fats. A half-cup serving provides nearly 100 percent of the recommended daily amount.

Turmeric is best known as a key ingredient in many curries. This bright yellow, bitter spice also has been used for years as an anti-inflammatory in treating both osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis due to high concentrations of curcumin.
